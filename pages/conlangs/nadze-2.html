Breaking Down the Phrases

"My name is..."

fill out later IPA Romanization Translation(s)
[r…Т…ЫћМ m…ТћМ…Є…Ы z«Р ТЋРn…ТЋР] rae mafe zijna My name is ...
  • The word "rae" translates to the first person nominative pronoun "I".
  • The second word, "mafe", translates to "to have/I have". The infinitive and first person present conjugations of any verb are always the same.
  • The final word, "zijna", translates to "name".

What is your name?

fill out later IPA Romanization Translation(s)
[ І«РnuЋР niЋС Фm…ТћМ…Є…Ы z«Р ТЋРn…ТЋР m…Ы] chinu ni'mafe zijna me What is your name?
  • "Chinu" translates to "what".
  • "Ni'mafe" is the present tense second person singular conjugation of "mafe". It means "you have".
  • "me" is a particle denoting a question. Particles go at the end of the sentence.
